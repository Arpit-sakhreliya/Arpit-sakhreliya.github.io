About Me

I'm a graduate student in Electrical & Computer Engineering with a focus on VLSI design, verification, and accelerator architectures. I have worked on RTL implementations, cache and memory system projects, FPGA-based prototypes, and ASIC tapeout flows during internships and academic work.

Journey

My VLSI journey started with digital logic and progressed to building synthesizable RTL, performing timing closure, and learning full ASIC flows. I enjoy optimizing microarchitecture for performance and power, and bringing complex designs from algorithm to silicon.
