Approach

Engineering Philosophy

Reliability First

Hardware must work — not just in the lab, but in the field. I design with worst-case margins, robust fault handling, and extensive validation because a system that fails in production has already failed its purpose.

Full-Stack Thinking

The best hardware engineers understand the entire stack. From transistor-level design through firmware and system architecture — every layer informs the others, and optimization happens at the boundaries.

Measure Everything

In God we trust; all others must bring data. Oscilloscope traces, thermal profiles, timing reports — quantitative validation beats intuition every time. If you can't measure it, you can't improve it.
